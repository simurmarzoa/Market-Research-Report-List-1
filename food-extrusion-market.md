<p><ul><li>Cold extrusion</li><li>Hot extrusion</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The food extrusion market is primarily divided into cold and hot extrusion processes. Cold extrusion involves the use of high pressure to shape materials without applying heat, ideal for delicate products like snack bars and protein-rich foods. In contrast, hot extrusion utilizes heat to melt and mix ingredients, producing a variety of textures and flavors in products such as pasta, cereals, and meat substitutes. Both methods cater to distinct consumer preferences and applications in the food industry.</p></p>
